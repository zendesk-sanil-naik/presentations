🎬 Scenario Time: Good, Bad, or Ugly?

Vote on these real meeting scenarios:

Scenario 1: The Last-Minute Agenda

Sarah sends a meeting agenda... 5 minutes before the meeting starts. Is this:

VERDICT: Bad! Better than nothing, but send agendas 24-48 hours ahead so people can prepare properly.

Scenario 2: The 15-Person Brainstorm

You invite all 15 team members to brainstorm a new feature. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! Brainstorms work best with 3-7 people. With 15, you'll get groupthink and most people won't contribute.

Scenario 3: The Standup Email

The team decides to do async standups via Slack instead of meetings. Is this:

VERDICT: Good! Async standups can work great for distributed teams. The key is consistency and actually reading what people post.

Scenario 4: The 25-Minute Default

Raj books meetings for 25 minutes instead of 30, so everyone gets a breather before their next call. Is this:

VERDICT: Good! Shorter defaults force focus, cut Parkinson's Law padding, and give people time to reset between meetings.

Scenario 5: The Meeting With No Decision

After an hour of discussion, the meeting ends with "let's schedule another meeting to decide." Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! Every meeting should have a clear purpose and a decision-maker. If you can't decide, you likely lacked the right people or pre-reads.

Scenario 6: The Follow-Up Notes

Within an hour of the meeting, Priya shares notes with clear action items and owners. Is this:

VERDICT: Good! Notes with owners and deadlines turn talk into action and stop the same topics resurfacing next week.

Scenario 7: The Should-Have-Been-An-Email

A 30-minute meeting is booked to share information everyone could've read in 3 minutes. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! If there's no discussion or decision needed, send an email or a doc. Respect everyone's time. 📧

Scenario 8: The Meeting To Plan The Meeting

The team schedules a meeting to figure out what to discuss in the next meeting. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! Inception, but worse. Draft an agenda async — you don't need a meeting to prepare for a meeting. 🌀

Scenario 9: The No-Show Organizer

The person who called the meeting doesn't show up, leaving everyone else wondering why they're there. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! Peak chaos energy. If you own the meeting, show up and lead it — or cancel it. 👻

Scenario 10: The Zombie Recurring Meeting

A weekly sync has run for a year. No one remembers why it started, but it keeps happening. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! Audit recurring meetings regularly. If it has no clear purpose, kill it or make it async. 🧟

Scenario 11: The Meeting After The Meeting

The real discussion happens in a hallway or DM right after the meeting ends. Is this:

VERDICT: Ugly! If people don't feel safe speaking up in the room, that's a psychological-safety problem. Fix the meeting, not the hallway. 🚪

Scenario 12: Ignoring the Invite

An invite lands in your inbox and you never respond — no Accept, no Decline, no Tentative. You'll just decide on the day. Is this:

VERDICT: Bad! No response leaves the organizer blind — they can't tell if they'll have quorum or the right people. Always RSVP (Accept / Decline / Tentative) so they can plan or reschedule. 📨

🌟 The Four Core Principles

These aren't just nice-to-haves. They're the foundations:

🤝 Respectful

Everyone's time and input is valued. Active listening trumps speaking. Psychological safety allows honest dialogue.

🤲 Collaborative

We're solving problems together, not competing. Build on each other's ideas. Shared ownership of outcomes.

💬 Interactive

Engagement over presentation. Questions encouraged. Multiple voices heard. Silence means we're missing perspectives.

🌍 Inclusive

Everyone can contribute meaningfully. Diverse perspectives make better decisions. No one should be a spectator.

⚡ Rapid Fire: Meeting Hygiene Checklist

These apply to EVERY meeting. No exceptions!

✅ Before

  • Clear agenda 24-48hrs ahead
  • Right people invited
  • Required reading shared
  • Ask: "Can this be async?"

✅ During

  • Start and end on time
  • State the objective upfront
  • One conversation at a time
  • Invite quiet voices
  • Park off-topic items

✅ After

  • Share notes within 24hrs
  • Clear owners & deadlines
  • Follow up on actions
  • Close the feedback loop

✅ Always

  • Psychological safety first
  • Respect everyone's time
  • Active listening > talking
  • Celebrate what works

🚨 The Meeting Emergency Kit

When things go wrong (and they will), use these phrases:

🗣️ When someone dominates:

"Thanks Alex. I want to make sure we hear from everyone. Let's hear other perspectives."

🔥 When things get heated:

"Both views are valid. Let's take a 5-minute break and come back to this."

🎯 When going off-track:

"Important topic! How does this relate to today's goal of [X]? Let's park it for now."

🤐 When there's dead silence:

"I'll start. Here's what I'm thinking... [share]. What are your thoughts?"

⏰ When running out of time:

"We have 5 minutes left. Let's make a decision: what are we committing to?"
