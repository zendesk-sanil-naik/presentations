# Running Effective Meetings - Brown Bag Session

An interactive presentation on meeting hygiene and best practices for various meeting types.

## How to Use

1. Open `index.html` in any modern web browser
2. Navigate using:
   - **Arrow keys** (← →) or **Space bar**
   - **Previous/Next buttons** on screen
3. Interactive elements:
   - Click checklist items to mark them as complete
   - Click principle cards for visual feedback
   - All interactive elements are designed for engagement during the session

## Presentation Structure

### Part 1: Foundations (Slides 1-5)
- Introduction and why meetings matter
- The Four Core Principles: Respectful, Collaborative, Interactive, Inclusive
- Universal Meeting Hygiene checklist
- Meeting lifecycle (Before, During, After)
- Creating psychological safety

### Part 2: Meeting Types (Slides 6-10)
Specific guidance for:
- **Daily Standups** - Quick sync, blockers, momentum
- **Brainstorming** - Idea generation techniques
- **Sprint Planning & Refinement** - Agile ceremonies
- **Retrospectives** - Process improvement
- **Decision-Making** - Frameworks and models

### Part 3: Practical Application (Slides 11-17)
- Remote & hybrid meeting considerations
- Facilitator's toolkit with practical phrases
- Red flags and warning signs
- Quick wins you can implement tomorrow
- Interactive group exercise
- Team commitments and next steps

## Key Features

- **Checklist items** - Click to check/uncheck

## Facilitation Tips

### Timing (60-75 minutes total)
- Slides 1-5: 15 minutes (foundations)
- Slides 6-10: 20 minutes (meeting types)
- Slides 11-14: 15 minutes (practical tips)
- Slide 15: 10 minutes (group exercise)
- Slides 16-17: 10 minutes (commitments + Q&A)

### Group Exercise (Slide 15)
Break into groups of 3-5 people. Give each group:
1. 3 minutes for Question 1 (best meeting experience)
2. 3 minutes for Question 2 (frustrations and fixes)
3. 4 minutes for Question 3 (concrete action for next week)

Have each group share 1-2 key insights with the full team.

### Making It Interactive
- Pause at checklist slides and have people share examples
- On "Red Flags" slide, ask "Which of these sound familiar?"
- On "Quick Wins" slide, poll the team on which to try first
- Encourage questions throughout, not just at the end

- **Meeting types**: Add/remove meeting types relevant to your team
